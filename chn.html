Great Wall of China - China


• The Great Wall of China is a series of fortifications that were built across the historical northern borders of ancient Chinese states and Imperial China as protection against various nomadic groups from the Eurasian Steppe.

• Stretches over 21,196 km, with sections built over various dynasties for more than 1,500 years.

• Built as a defence mechanism to protect against invasions, primarily from nomadic tribes, and is equipped with watchtowers and gates, the wall was a complex network of defences.

• It took millions of labourers, soldiers, and prisoners to construct, with many perishing during its construction. The Great Wall remains a symbol of Chinese resilience and remains one of the most visited landmarks in China.

• It is considered the largest religious structure in the world by Guinness World Records.
