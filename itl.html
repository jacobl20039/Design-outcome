Colosseum - Italy


• The Colosseum is an elliptical amphitheatre in the centre of the city of Rome, Italy, just east of the Roman Forum. It is the largest ancient amphitheatre ever built, and is still the largest standing amphitheatre in the world, despite its age.

• It features an advanced design, with systems for trapdoors, elevators, and a retractable awning to shield spectators from the sun.

• It could host around 50,000 to 80,000 spectators who gathered to watch gladiator contests and public spectacles.

• The Colosseum has undergone multiple restorations and remains a key tourist attraction. It is also a UNESCO World Heritage Site and an emblem of ancient architectural prowess.

• Its construction symbolises the wealth and grandeur of ancient Rome.
