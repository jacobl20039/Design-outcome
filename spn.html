La Sagrada Familia - Spain


• The Basílica i Temple Expiatori de la Sagrada Família, otherwise known as Sagrada Família, is a church under construction in the Eixample district of Barcelona, Catalonia, Spain. It was designed by the renowned architect Antoni Gaudí, a major figure in the Catalan Modernisme movement.


• Begun in 1882, it’s still unfinished, with an anticipated completion around 2026, marking the centenary of Gaudí's death.


• The basilica features a mix of Gothic and Art Nouveau forms, especially noted in its towering spires and intricate facades.


• La Sagrada Familia is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a prominent symbol of Barcelona. Gaudí’s fusion of nature, faith, and geometry is unparalleled.


• Gaudí incorporated significant Christian symbolism into the structure, with each part of the church telling stories from the Bible.
